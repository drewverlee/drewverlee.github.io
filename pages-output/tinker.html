Packs and Promises

A tinker’s debt is always paid
Once for any simple trade.
Twice for freely-given aid.
Thrice for any insult made.

Abenthy claimed to be more than one; Felurian's description suggests their Faen and their universally respected. Except for when they're not, crazy Martin rightfully punched one for getting handsy with a child. We know how reputations can escape the truth, and there's is written down in a song older than God: "Tinker Tanner". We only hear a verse that a young boy made up, though Kvothe approves, about a young woman bathing on the other side of the river from all men. How this would fit into a song about what so far has been a boys club of traveling merchants were left to wonder.

As to their other occupation, We have yet to see them making leather from animal hides or selling it. Though lots of people in the story end up dead as leather. I'm left wondering if the name tanner hints at a historical mutation of glammer, a connection to their Faen origins. The inspiration for another famous song about making them leave the town.

Tinker's could be Thinkers, old Knowers, which would fit given they seem to have a knack for having what our young hero needs that would make the Cthaeh jealous. Offered at prices always conspicuously within reach, as if what matters aren't the prices but then pushing events in the right direction. Making Kvothe might be more of a carrier~donkey~jackass~lacklass than he realizes, moving the items to their proper place. Much like Auri does in the Underthing, which she knows as well as a Tinker's packs.

Is it possible our laden friends tinker, like Abenthy, in dark arts better left alone? They certainly seem to run into troubled young men quite often: Jax and Kvothe. Which, if our previous guess is correct, means Jax managed to offend Knowing, Seeing, and Listening in his pursuit of the moon. Leaving the Tinker to mend the broken house, which we assume to be the four corners, given all the work they seem to be doing here. Which would make Jax's new home, woven from whole cloth, the Fae? But then, if as so many have speculated, Jax was then shut beyond the doors of stone... would that put him back in the four corners? That would put us in quite the theoretical Jax-in-box, wouldn't it? I wouldn't pay it much mind. It's a theory as cracked as Jax's clay cup... or was it a mug? Either way, broken.

Tinker
Rumble, ramble, thistle caught
Can you mend this shoe, like new bought?

Finder, Grinder
Stone stabbed, kicked and knaed
Can you restore this blade, sharp bright lite before?

Knower, Seer, Listener
Numb, cold, bloodless and betrayed
Can you heal this heart, make music beat sweet once more?
